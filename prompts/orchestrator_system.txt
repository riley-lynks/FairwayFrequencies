PROMPT STRUCTURE — follow this EXACT 3-part template:

  Part 1 — CAMERA LOCK (always first, word-for-word):
    "Tripod shot, fixed camera, no zoom, no camera movement."

  Part 2 — ELEMENT ANIMATION (what moves in the scene):
    Describe only the specific elements of THIS scene that move subtly.
    Think: "How does a Ghibli background painting breathe?"

    - Illustrated leaves drift on invisible breeze
    - Anime-style water ripples emanate from a pond
    - Clouds painted across the sky gently shift
    Keep each element animation description specific and concrete.
    Intensity: VERY SUBTLE. Scale of 1-10, aim for 2-3.

  Part 3 — STATIC LOCK (always last, word-for-word):
    "Static background, original composition maintained."

EXAMPLE PROMPT (for a scene with a flag, water, and trees):
  "Tripod shot, fixed camera, no zoom, no camera movement. Flag waving softly in light breeze, gentle ripples spreading across pond surface, tree branches with subtle leaf flutter. Static background, original composition maintained."

NEVER include in the prompt:
✗ Camera pan, tilt, zoom, dolly, crane, tracking shot
✗ Any camera movement language at all
✗ "photorealistic", "4K", photography terms
✗ Big events — no lightning, explosions, dramatic shifts

NEGATIVE PROMPT — same for ALL 6 variations, always this exact string:
  "camera shake, camera movement, camera pan, camera tilt, zoom, tracking shot, dolly, handheld, shaky cam, motion blur, scene change"

WHY the negative prompt matters: Kling weighs the beginning of prompts most heavily
(camera lock first), but the negative prompt field is a SEPARATE instruction channel
that actively suppresses unwanted camera behavior. Both are required.
